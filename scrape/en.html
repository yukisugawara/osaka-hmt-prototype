Using Knowledge in the Humanities
to Flexibly Adapt
to Change

Overview of the Graduate School of Humanities

　Society, the world, and Japan keep on changing, but we are not always able to successfully adapt to such changes in our environment.
　Even though technology, industry, and medicine are evolving toward creating a safer and more comfortable world, more people are becoming anxious and exhausted, as well as feeling uncomfortable with and angry at social fragmentation.
　The friction resulting from innate human nature and behavior cannot be resolved simply through developments in technology and institutional reform. It is exactly such issues that need to be addressed by the humanities as well as the social sciences. In this critical situation, we believe it is our mission to apply our profound knowledge in the humanities to address those social issues, as well as take that knowledge to an even higher level.
　It is against this backdrop that we have decided to launch the Graduate School of Humanities. We will conduct more comprehensive, international education and research in the humanities with the goal of contributing to the formation of a new society, all the while adapting to social needs and building on traditional knowledge in the humanities.

Divisions

  • Division of Humanities
    In this department,
    we delve into human nature and behavior that remain unchanged over time,
    explore literature and history in depth and learn from the past.

  • Division of Language and Culture
    This division connects language,
    culture and information technology in a systematic manner,
    promoting education that integrates the humanities and sciences.

  • Division of Foreign Studies
    This division aims to develop individuals who can succeed internationally by leveraging their advanced language proficiency and deep understanding of cultures and regions where that language is spoken.

  • Division of Japanese Studies
    This division aims to develop broad-based knowledge in Japanese language, culture, and society, and fosters individuals who have both fundamental and applied research skills in Japanese studies.

  • Division of Arts Studies
    This is one of the largest divisions of arts studies among those of Japanese national universities in which students can learn about a broad range of fields in the arts, aesthetic theories, and much more.

Four Missions of the Graduate School of Humanities

  1. Develop individuals who can address current issues with determination and succeed internationally
  2. Develop new fields in the humanities
  3. Make humanities in the form of liberal arts education accessible and available to students
  4. Make advanced knowledge in the humanities accessible to society in general
